Ask ten viewers why one animation feels *premium* and another feels *cheap*, and few will mention timing. Yet timing is almost always the difference. Great motion graphics blend **rhythm, timing, and story** — and the good news is that timing is a skill you can learn.

## Timing is everything

Nothing in the real world starts or stops instantly. Objects accelerate, carry momentum, and settle. Animation feels natural when it obeys the same physics, and that behaviour comes from **easing curves**.

> Linear motion is the fastest way to make expensive software look like a 2005 PowerPoint. Ease everything.

### Choose the right easing

Each curve has a personality. Pick the one that matches the emotion you want:

| Easing | Feels like | Use it for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `power2.out` | Calm, refined | UI reveals, text fades |
| `power4.out` | Snappy, confident | Hero entrances |
| `back.out` | Playful overshoot | Buttons, badges |
| `elastic.out` | Bouncy, fun | Logos, mascots |

## A reusable GSAP snippet

Here is a clean, production-friendly reveal you can drop into any project:

```js
// Staggered, eased reveal with GSAP
gsap.from(".reveal", {
  y: 40,
  opacity: 0,
  duration: 0.9,
  ease: "power4.out",
  stagger: 0.08,
})
```

Two details make it feel professional: the **stagger** (elements arrive in sequence, not all at once) and the **out** easing (fast start, gentle landing).

## Principles that matter most

Of Disney's twelve principles of animation, these carry the most weight in motion graphics:

1. **Ease in / ease out** — never move at a constant speed.
2. **Anticipation** — a tiny pull-back before a big move.
3. **Follow-through** — let elements settle, don't stop them dead.
4. **Staging** — one clear focal point per moment.
