Most Reels don't fail because of the algorithm. They fail in the **first second** — before the algorithm ever decides to push them. If people swipe away instantly, nothing else you do matters.

Here's how we approach Reels that reach beyond your existing followers.

## The first 3 seconds decide everything

Your hook has one job: stop the scroll. The strongest hooks do at least one of these:

- Show the **result** first, then explain how.
- Ask a question the viewer *needs* answered.
- Start mid-action — no logos, no slow intro.
- Put bold on-screen text in the top third.

> If you can't describe your hook in one sentence, your viewer can't grasp it in one second.

## Get the specs right

Technical mistakes quietly kill reach. Match these before you export:

| Setting | Recommended |
| --- | --- |
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 (vertical) |
| Resolution | 1080 × 1920 |
| Length | 7–15s for reach, up to 30s for depth |
| Safe zone | Keep text away from the bottom 15% |
| Audio | Trending or original, always captioned |

## Editing rhythm

Retention is a rhythm game. Keep the viewer moving:

1. **Cut on motion.** Every 1–2 seconds, change something.
2. **Caption everything.** Most people watch on mute.
3. **Loop it.** End where you began so the replay feels seamless.
4. **One idea per Reel.** Save the second idea for the next post.

## Your pre-publish checklist

- [x] Hook lands in under 1 second
- [x] Vertical 1080 × 1920 export
- [x] Captions burned in
- [x] Clear call to action at the end
- [x] Cover frame chosen on purpose
